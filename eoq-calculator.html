What is Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)?

Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) is the ideal order quantity a company should purchase to minimise its total inventory costs — balancing the cost of ordering too frequently against the cost of holding too much stock. It was developed by Ford W. Harris in 1913 and remains a cornerstone of supply chain management.

The EOQ Formula

EOQ = √( 2DS / H )

  • D — Annual demand (units per year)
  • S — Ordering cost per order (€ per order)
  • H — Holding cost per unit per year (€ per unit/year)

The formula finds the point where ordering costs and holding costs are exactly equal — the mathematical minimum of total inventory cost.

Ordering Cost vs Holding Cost

Cost TypeWhat's IncludedBehaviour
Ordering CostAdmin, processing, shipping, inspection per orderDecreases as order size increases
Holding CostStorage, insurance, obsolescence, capital tied upIncreases as order size increases
Total CostSum of bothU-shaped curve — minimum at EOQ

EOQ Assumptions and Limitations

The classic EOQ model assumes:

  • Demand is constant and known throughout the year
  • Lead time is constant and known
  • No quantity discounts apply
  • No stockouts are allowed

In reality, demand fluctuates. This is why EOQ is used alongside safety stock and reorder point calculations for a complete inventory management system.

How to Apply EOQ in Practice

  • Calculate EOQ for each SKU independently
  • Review holding costs annually — storage rates change
  • Combine with safety stock to handle demand variability
  • Use reorder point = (average daily demand × lead time) + safety stock
