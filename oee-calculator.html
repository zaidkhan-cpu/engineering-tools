What is OEE and Why Does It Matter?

Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) is the gold standard metric for measuring manufacturing productivity. Developed from Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) principles, OEE tells you how effectively a machine or production line is being used compared to its full potential.

The OEE Formula

OEE is calculated by multiplying three components, each as a percentage:

  • Availability — Percentage of scheduled time the machine was actually running. Reduced by breakdowns and changeovers.
  • Performance — How fast the machine ran compared to its ideal speed. Reduced by micro-stops and slow cycles.
  • Quality — Percentage of parts produced without defects. Reduced by rejects, rework, and scrap.

If all three are 90%, your OEE is only 0.9 × 0.9 × 0.9 = 72.9% — far lower than it appears. This is why OEE is such a revealing metric.

How to Use OEE to Find Losses

The real power of OEE is identifying which component is the weakest link:

  • Low Availability? Focus on predictive maintenance and faster changeovers (SMED).
  • Low Performance? Analyse cycle times, operator pacing, and machine speed settings.
  • Low Quality? Implement SPC (Statistical Process Control) and root cause analysis on defects.
